DURING

Fundamentals never ever ever ever ever go out of style

Dedicate yourself to rigorous repetition of fundamentals. Negect nothing. Learn everything. Be the tortoise, not the hare.

CHROME DEV TOOLS

Getting comfortable here is everything as a front-end developer. You will spend more time here than you will in the code.
Learn how it all works.

Be a Git boss

There is no better way to appear instantly knowledgeable, and to be immediately useful, than to have command of version control.

Do not make Git look like Guitar Hero!

Commit to how Cf teaches Agile

Agile workflow can take a lot of forms. What is taught here is a middle-strict path, IMHO. Embrace it. Being comfortable in your first standups at a new job immediately conveys your readiness.

It is OK to ask

Your instructors and TAs are here to help you, and the most direct way they can do that is to answer your questions. So, by all means, ask!!!
(Same thing applies on the job!)

Be attentive to UI/UX

Think about a time you used an interface that sucked.
Think about an interface that was awesome.
Look at your own project as someone new to it. What would they think?

CF is way more intense than the gig

The job is not as hard as this. There are some incredibly hard moments. There are bad days. There are some moments you feel useless and lost. CF is harder than all of those.

Take care of yourself

Right now you are doing one of the hardest things you will ever do. You are better equipped for it when you take care of yourself. Sleep. Food. Hydrate. Exercise. Breaks.

Keep with the networking!

Take seriously the networking parts of career coaching. Do the coffee meetings. Go to the meetups. Do lots of both of them. It matters a lot.

TAKE CAREER COACHING SERIOUSLY

Coding is only part of the job. Career coaching gets you ready for the rest of it.

You can do this

You can.
